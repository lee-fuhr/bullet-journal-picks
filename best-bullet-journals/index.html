A good bullet journal is more than a notebook, it’s a system for organizing your time, thoughts, and goals. The best ones combine a durable cover, a pleasant dot grid, and smart extras like numbered pages and an index, all at a paper weight that suits your pen. Whether you’re a fountain pen aficionado or a minimalist list-maker, the right journal can make the difference between a hobby that sticks and one that collects dust. When choosing, pay attention to paper thickness (heavier sheets ghost less), page count (most journals offer 200–250 pages), and cover type (hardcover for durability, softcover for portability). Extras such as a pen loop, two bookmarks, and a pre-printed key can save setup time.
